About Me

I started my career in engineering with a simple project. Making a probability game in Microsoft Excel. As I continued, the projects got more complex and covered more areas of interest. Eventually, I joined my high school’s FIRST Robotics Team, and together we worked to complete a larger project. I worked in pneumatics, networking, electronics, and modeling, the entire time learning from and teaching others. It is what opened my eyes to what can be achieved when multiple heads are put together.

In college, the projects did not stop. In my classes, I took the group projects and went over the top. I joined The Underwater Robotics Team and helped lead them to the semi-finals. I held the position of electrical lead, vice president, and technical advisor. This time there was nobody to show me the way. I was trailblazing and passing down the information that I discovered.

In my professional work, I hold the same passion. I see it as another chance to learn and gain new perspectives. I always try to bring experiences from my past and try to find any new ways to look at a problem. I always have and will continue to take great pride in what I do.
