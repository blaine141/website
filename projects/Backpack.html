My Backpack

October 2012 - May 2016

    All throughout my high school career, I have been adding features to my backpack. It all started freshman year when I thought it would be cool to have a usb port on my backpack. My mom had a simple portable battery and I came up with the idea to put that battery in my backpack and mount a usb port in the headphone hole in my backpack. It only went on from there. 

    Now that I had this usb port, I needed cables. I started packing my backpack with headphones, audio cables, charging cables, and communication cables. Along with extra batteries.

     Next, I added speakers to my backpack. My dad supplied me with a 12v 8000mAh NiMH battery and I just grabbed the set of desktop speakers that I had at home. I wanted the speakers to be bluetooth so I got a bluetooth module to wirelessly recieve audio and play it through the speakers.

    About this time I learned about the Raspberry Pi. My dad bought one immediately and asked for me to learn how to use it for him. I had used linux before, but never that much. Once I finally got the Raspberry Pi running, I put it in my backpack to use at school for assignments and other things. I got a portable screen and a wireless keyboard. About this time I also made my homework webpage. It is just a list of my homework assignments. Then, at school, to add assignments to the list, I would pull out the wireless keyboard and type in the assignment. This would type the assignment into the webpage and submit it to the list. This was how I recorded homework assignments before I created I.R.I.S.

    After a while without many upgrades, I thought to add an outlet to my backpack. Everyone said that I had everything, but I realized that I did not have ac power. I found a 12v inverter online and ordered it. In just a few days, I was walking around my house with my backpack on and carrying a lamp that it was powering.

    Then came the idea that I am most well known for in my school. I was sitting in psycology, junior year, wondering what else I could add to my backpack. I was thinking of decorative lights, but I couldn't think of exactly what I wanted. Then, suddenly, the idea hit me. Turn signals! As soon as I got home, I got to work researching. My dad provided me a microchip PIC18f4685 demo board and introduced me to the programming language C. I had a prototype set up in 3 days and was interested to see the amazed looks on everyones faces. I had technical issues with that demo board so he provided me the circut board that the buisness where he works produces. I made a few modifications to the hardware and reprogrammed it. mounted lights on the back and switches to the front straps. Version one was completed. 

    Over the summer of 2016, I gained more experience with PIC microcontrollers and the language of C while working for Electrimotion. Electrimotion produces a device that contains a PIC microchip, a screen, keypad, and many inputs and outputs. So, I immediately wanted to use it for my backpack. So, that summer, I took that device and modified it to be my new backpack controller. Now, with this new screen and keypad, I was capable of changing settings about the turn signals and control power to the outlet. I was also able to read voltage, temperature, and the ammount of current being drawn from my backpack battery and how many mAh have been drawn from the battery since the last charge. 

    Now, I walk around school with all the technical supplies I need including a multi-meter and screw drivers, any time people need power, they come to me, and I travel the school signaling my turns to make the hallways a better and safer place.
