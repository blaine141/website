The F150

March 2014 - May 2014

    My freshman year in highschool, I joined the Olentangy High School Robotics Team. My dad joined as a chaperone and we both had a blast that year. He got really involved and we were both really disappointed when it ended. The next few days he was telling me all about other robot controllers that there are. Soon, he had an idea.

    The idea was to take a Power Wheels car and make it remote controlled. We got a Gorgon from 221 Robotic Systems. This is a programmable device with many outputs able to recieve inputs through wifi. To control the motors, we got Talon speed controllers. They get PWM input and vary the speed of motors based off of that input. Then to turn the steering wheel, we needed position feedback to know where the steering wheel was positioned. We put in a potentiometer and connected it to the steering colums to monitor where the wheel was turned. Then, it was simple to program it and set up the drive station. Using a simple joystick and a laptop, I could make the truck drive and turn. 

    Also, at one point, I added a model rocket launcher to the truck. We then drove it out into the middle of a field and launched a rocket from hundreds of feet away at the press of a button. It was pretty cool!
