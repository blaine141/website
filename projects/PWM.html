PWM Generator

- February 2017

       My robotics instructor asked me to make a PWM generator with a screen on it showing it's current setting to controll variable speed motor controllers. I started development by looking at PWM protocol. It passes data based on the width of a pulse. In the case of the motor controllers we use, a 1ms pulse is full speed negative and it is a linear relationship to a 2ms pulse being full speed forward with a 1.5ms pulse being stopped.

        The microcontroller I used did not have enough outputs to control two digits of 7-segment displays so my dad tought me a little trick. You put power on the positive leds for each segment and tie it to the leds on the other digit. Then you output power to make the digit you want and ground the digit you want to display. You just then quickly pulse between digits.

        My instructor wanted me to work with other people on the project so I had a kid 3d print a case for the device. Once we had cases made, I worked with about 5 other kids in an assembly line format to make more while teaching them how it all works.
